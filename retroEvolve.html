The 1990s Era

1990-1999

The 90s saw the transition from 2D to 3D gaming, with iconic consoles like the Super Nintendo, PlayStation, and Nintendo 64. This era introduced:

  • First mainstream 3D graphics
  • Birth of famous franchises (Mario 64, Final Fantasy VII)
  • Arcade culture at its peak
  • Introduction of CD-ROM technology

This decade established many gameplay conventions we still see today.

The 2000s Era

2000-2009

The 2000s brought online gaming to the mainstream and HD graphics with consoles like Xbox 360 and PS3. Key developments:

  • Xbox Live revolutionized online multiplayer
  • Motion controls (Wii) changed how we play
  • Handheld gaming flourished (Nintendo DS, PSP)
  • Open-world games became popular

This era saw gaming become a dominant entertainment medium.

The 2010s Era

2010-2019

The 2010s were about connectivity and immersion with PS4, Xbox One, and Nintendo Switch. Notable trends:

  • Digital distribution became standard
  • Esports grew into a billion-dollar industry
  • Virtual Reality made consumer breakthroughs
  • Indie games gained mainstream success

Gaming became more social and accessible than ever before.

The Modern Era

2020-Present

Today's gaming features cutting-edge technology with PS5, Xbox Series X, and cloud gaming. Current highlights:

  • Ray tracing and 4K/8K graphics
  • Cloud gaming services (Game Pass, GeForce Now)
  • Cross-platform play becoming standard
  • Handheld PCs (Steam Deck) gaining popularity

We're seeing the lines between console, PC, and mobile gaming blur.

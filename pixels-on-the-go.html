Game & Watch
1980

Nintendo's pioneering series of handheld electronic games, featuring simple LCD gameplay and dual-functioning as a clock/alarm. These compact, single-game devices laid the foundation for portable gaming.

Game Boy
1989

Nintendo's iconic handheld console that popularized portable gaming with classics like Tetris and Pok√©mon. Its durable design, long battery life, and massive game library made it a cultural phenomenon.

Game Gear
1990

Sega's ambitious color handheld rival to the Game Boy, featuring a backlit screen and impressive graphics for its time. Despite its power, its shorter battery life and smaller game library limited its success.

Game Boy Color
1998

Nintendo's first major handheld upgrade, adding vibrant color to the classic Game Boy experience. It introduced enhanced graphics and exclusive titles while maintaining backward compatibility.

Nintendo DS
2004

Revolutionized handheld gaming with its dual-screen design, touch controls, and backward compatibility. It became one of the best-selling consoles ever thanks to iconic titles like Mario Kart DS.

PlayStation Portable (PSP)
2004

Sony's groundbreaking handheld, blending high-end graphics, multimedia features, and robust hardware into a portable format. Redefined expectations for mobile gaming beyond traditional handhelds.

Nintendo 3DS
2011

Revolutionized handheld gaming with its glasses-free 3D display, dual screens, and augmented reality features. It built on the DS legacy with enhanced graphics and a robust library.

PlayStation Vita
2011

Sony's advanced handheld, featuring high-end graphics, dual analog sticks, and cross-platform connectivity. Despite impressive hardware, it faced challenges competing with mobile gaming.

Steam Deck
2022

Valve's powerful handheld PC, designed to play your entire Steam library on the go with console-like ease. Its customizable controls and robust hardware make it a dream for portable PC gamers.
